How to use

Slower. Cleaner. More deliberate.

The 1.5mm ritual is intentionally slower than the 0.5mm. Use only as directed and allow more recovery time between sessions.

01

Cleanse

Start with freshly cleansed, dry skin and clean hands. Reset the surface first.

02

Sanitize

Sanitize the roller thoroughly before use and allow to air-dry. Cleanliness is non-negotiable.

03

Use deliberately

Slow, measured passes. Do not press hard. Avoid broken, irritated or uncomfortable skin entirely.

04

Recover

Sanitize, dry, return to the holder. Allow more time between sessions than with a starter roller.

Use & care

Higher intensity means more discipline.

The 1.5mm depth asks for a slower, more careful cadence. Stay clean. Stay gentle. Stop if anything feels off — and always use as directed.

Before use

Wash hands. Cleanse and dry the skin. Sanitize the roller and allow it to air-dry before it touches your skin.

During use

Use slow, measured passes with light pressure. Do not press hard. Avoid broken, irritated, inflamed or uncomfortable skin. Stop if discomfort occurs.

After use & recovery

Sanitize again, rinse, and let it dry fully. Return it to the included holder. Build in more recovery time than you would for a starter roller.

Personal-use safety note

Use only as directed. Do not use on broken, irritated, inflamed or uncomfortable skin. Do not share personal-use tools. Stop use if discomfort occurs. If unsure, consult a qualified skincare professional.

Frequently asked

Important to know before you use 1.5mm.

Is this the right pick if I'm new to derma rolling?

No. Start with the 0.5mm. The 1.5mm is intended for experienced users who already understand cadence, recovery and personal-use safety.

How often should I use it?

Less often than a starter roller. Use only as directed, leave appropriate recovery time, and never rush the schedule.

How do I clean and store it?

Sanitize before and after each use, dry fully, and return to the clear storage holder. Keep the holder dry between uses.

What if my skin feels uncomfortable?

Stop immediately. Do not use on broken, irritated, inflamed or uncomfortable skin. Consult a qualified skincare professional if unsure.
